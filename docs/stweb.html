Results

CuP (Completion under Policy) counts a task as successful only if it is completed and no safety policy is violated. The ST-WebAgentBench paper shows that existing agents lose roughly a third of their completions to policy violations, with an average CuP of ~15% across three SOTA agents. TTGOS Guard achieves 46.4%, nearly 2x the best published result (AWM 23.8%), through a same-model ablation that isolates the Guard's contribution: +27.4 points over baseline.

Why This Matters

A safety layer that increases task completion

Safety mechanisms are typically expected to reduce performance: the agent does less in order to stay safe. TTGOS Guard does the opposite. Under identical conditions (same model, same benchmark lock, same step budget), adding the Guard raised CuP from 19.0% to 46.4%. The agent completed 23 additional tasks it would otherwise have failed. The Guard's structured per-step evaluation (ACT, PROBE, SAFE_ABORT) helps the agent navigate policy-laden tasks more effectively, not just more safely.

Nearly 2x the best published result

The official leaderboard lists AWM at 23.8% CuP on the Core benchmark (the best known result). TTGOS Guard achieves 46.4% on the same Core-84 task set. The other published agents score 11.3% (WebVoyager) and 11.4% (WorkArena).

CuP is a hard metric by design

CuP credits a task only when it is fully completed and every safety policy is satisfied. A single policy violation zeros the score for that task. The ST-WebAgentBench paper (IBM Research) reports that existing agents lose roughly a third of their completions to policy violations. The benchmark spans 6 safety dimensions including user consent, boundary enforcement, jailbreak resistance, and error handling.

How TTGOS Guard Works

TTGOS Guard is an independent safety layer that wraps any AI agent. It does not modify the underlying model; it monitors, evaluates, and intervenes at each step.

1 Agent proposes action The underlying model decides what to do next
→
2 Guard evaluates risk Policy-based risk assessment at every step
→
3 Decision ACT (safe), PROBE (investigate), or SAFE_ABORT (prevent harm)

About

TTGOS (Two-Topos Grounding OS) is built on an original mathematical theory that models agent safety as two separate layers (what the agent observes, and how it reasons) connected by a formal structure called a grounding morphism. This defines what a safe action is at each step, and proves that safety composes: if each step preserves grounding, the whole sequence does too.

The Guard is an operational implementation of this theorem. Because safety comes from structural invariants, not the model's own judgment, it works regardless of which model powers the agent.

TTGOS is a non-profit personal research project by Hironao Nakamura (independent researcher).

This is benchmark-scoped conformance evidence, not a general safety certification for deployment.
